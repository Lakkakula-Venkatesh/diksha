Our Fertility Treatments

We offer a full range of simple and advanced reproductive technologies to fit your needs.

IVF (Test Tube Baby)

In Vitro Fertilization is where the egg and sperm are joined in the lab, outside the body. The resulting embryo is then placed back into the womb.

Best for:

  • Blocked or missing fallopian tubes.
  • Unexplained infertility after other treatments.

ICSI (Micro-Fertilization)

Intra Cytoplasmic Sperm Injection is when a single sperm is injected directly into each egg using a special machine (Micromanipulator).

Best for:

  • Very low sperm count or poor quality sperm.
  • Previous failure of fertilization with standard IVF.

IUI (Artificial Insemination)

Intrauterine Insemination is a simpler technique where processed, high-quality sperm are placed directly into the uterus at the time of ovulation.

Best for:

  • Men with low sperm count (mildly).
  • Women with hostile cervical mucus.
  • Unexplained infertility (often tried first).

Egg & Embryo Donation

We help women who cannot use their own eggs (e.g., due to age 40+ or premature failure) by using screened donor eggs/embryos.

Best for:

  • Women over 40-42 years old.
  • Premature ovarian failure.
  • Repeated IVF failures.

Semen Banking

Sperm is collected, processed, and safely frozen in liquid nitrogen for long-term storage or use with donor samples (AID).

Best for:

  • Before vasectomy or cancer treatment (chemotherapy/radiation).
  • Severe male infertility requiring donor sperm (AID).

Blastocyst Culture

Embryos are grown for 5 days (instead of 2-3) to reach the Blastocyst stage before transfer. This helps us select the strongest embryos.

Best for:

  • Patients with failed IVF cycles before.
  • When doing Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT).

Common Questions

When is a couple considered to have an infertility problem?
A couple is considered infertile if they have had one year of unprotected intercourse without conception. The issue affects about 10 to 15% of all couples.
What are the most common causes of infertility?
Infertility often has multiple causes. The main factors are Male factor (40%), Uterine and Tubal factor (30%), and Ovulatory factor (<20%).
How does the woman's age affect pregnancy chances?
The number of infertile couples rises significantly with age. The spontaneous abortion rate also increases from 10% (under 30) to 34% (early 40s), making age a critical factor.
Who should choose IVF (In Vitro Fertilization)?
IVF is best if fallopian tubes are blocked or absent, for unexplained infertility that failed routine treatments, or for patients opting for egg/embryo donation.
Why is ICSI (Micro-Fertilization) needed instead of standard IVF?
ICSI is used for severely low sperm counts (less than 5 million per ml), poor quality sperms, or when fertilization failed in a previous IVF cycle. One sperm is injected per egg.
What is the success rate for IUI (Artificial Insemination)?
The success rate for IUI is generally 15-20% per cycle. It is highest for patients with cervical factor issues or those with severely low semen counts (less than 10 million per ml).
Who qualifies for Egg Donation?
Women over 40-42 years old, those in perimenopause/menopause, patients who have failed multiple IVF cycles, or those with premature ovarian failure can opt for egg donation.
What is the main benefit of Embryo Donation?
Embryo donation has the highest pregnancy rate (35-40%) because it uses excellent quality embryos. It is a preferred and often more cost-effective option compared to full egg donation.
How safe is semen from the Semen Bank?
Frozen samples are quarantined for six months in liquid nitrogen. The donor is retested for infections like HIV and Hepatitis before the sample is released, minimizing disease risk.
Is there any risk with Blastocyst Culture?
Yes, in 20-30% of patients, none of the embryos may develop into a Blastocyst, resulting in no embryo transfer. Patients are counseled on this risk before the procedure.
